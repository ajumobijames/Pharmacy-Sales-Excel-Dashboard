##  Project Overview

This project presents a **Pharmacy Sales Dashboard** designed to provide stakeholders with a clear, data-driven view of business performance across multiple European pharmacy locations. The dashboard enables decision-makers to monitor key performance indicators (KPIs), identify top-performing products and stores, understand customer ordering behavior, and track monthly revenue trends — all from a single, interactive interface.

The dashboard covers five product categories:
- **Medical Devices**
- **OTC (Over-the-Counter)**
- **Personal Care**
- **Prescription**
- **Wellness**

##  Dataset Description

The dataset contains transactional pharmacy sales records across European HealthPoint store locations. Key fields include:

- **Order date & time** — used to derive weekday vs. weekend ordering patterns and monthly trends
- **Store location** — city and location type (Urban, Suburban, Rural)
- **Product/Brand** — brand name and product category
- **Revenue, Cost & Profit** — financial metrics per transaction
- **Units Sold & Order Count** — volume metrics

## Analysis & Analytics

### 1. Revenue & Profitability
Total revenue of **€8.6M** against a cost base of **€6.2M** yields a gross profit of **€2.4M**, representing a profit margin of approximately **27.9%**.

### 2. Brand Performance
**PainLess and Medica** record the highest order volumes among the top 5 brands, making them priority SKUs for inventory and marketing investment.

### 3. Customer Ordering Behavior
**75.6% of all orders are placed on weekdays**, indicating a routine and prescription-driven customer base. Weekend promotions could help close this gap.

### 4. Store Performance
**Munich HealthPoint #095** leads all stores significantly. 

### 5. Geographic Distribution
**Milan and Hamburg** top city-level sales, while Munich's city-wide figures are lower despite housing the best individual store — indicating concentrated, not broad, city performance.

### 6. Sales by Location Type
**Urban stores account for 47.8%** of sales, with suburban at 36% — a significant secondary market worth targeting for expansion.

### 7. Monthly Sales Trend
**Peak sales occur between May and July**, with dips in early Q1. Aligning inventory and staffing around this seasonal pattern is key to operational efficiency.

##  Conclusion

The Pharmacy Sales Dashboard delivers a comprehensive view of sales performance across European HealthPoint locations. Key takeaways:

- **Munich HealthPoint #095** is the standout performer and a model for underperforming branches
- **Weekday dominance (75.6%)** points to an opportunity to drive weekend engagement
- **Urban locations generate nearly half of all sales**, but suburban (36%) shows strong growth potential
- **May–July is the sales peak** — aligning resources during this window maximizes revenue
- **PainLess and Medica** should be priority SKUs for stock and promotional focus
